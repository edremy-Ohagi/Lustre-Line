# Mobile UX Rules

## Primary Assumption

Most visitors are likely to access the site from mobile phones. Mobile is the primary experience.

## Mobile Hard Requirements

- Design mobile-first, then expand to desktop.
- The first screen must show brand identity, jewelry atmosphere, and a clear consultation action.
- Header must not occupy too much vertical space.
- Buttons must be easy to tap.
- Product cards must remain readable on narrow screens.
- Text must not overflow, overlap, or become too small.
- Product detail modal must work comfortably on mobile.
- Independent product detail page must be easy to read on mobile.
- Contact / QR code sections must not crowd the screen.

## Interaction Rules

- Use clear CTA text such as `立即咨询`, `查看详情`, `询问价格`.
- Keep category filters reachable and horizontally scrollable if needed.
- Avoid hover-only interactions.
- Avoid dense desktop-style tables.
- Prefer bottom-friendly flows for detail and consultation actions.

## Performance Rules

- Optimize image dimensions.
- Use lazy loading where appropriate.
- Avoid heavy animation.
- Keep first screen fast.

## Visual Rules On Mobile

- Do not overuse large hero typography that pushes products too far below the fold.
- Show a hint of product content early.
- Keep enough whitespace, but avoid making the page feel empty.
- Ensure QR codes and contact blocks are large enough to scan or read.
